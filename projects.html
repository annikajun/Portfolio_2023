LearnBetter
"LearnBetter" is an app that functions as a Virtual Learning Environment to assist college students during remote studying. The app was created as part of a group project during my first year as a Digital Media Design student utilizing Mural and Figma. "LearnBetter" consists of eight features which can be seen on the second picture on the left. Among other things, "LearnBetter" helps students track their performance, lets them reach out and receive the required help, limits other phone activities, and provides a virtual group learning option. Its main purpose is to limit distractions from other phone features and promote motivation. Furthermore, the app supports students with different resources so they can get the help needed even when studying remotely.







Musicians Website
My website is about 5 Seconds of Summer, or 5SOS, an Australian pop-rock band. They formed in late 2011, and I have supported them since mid-2012. I have had a problem with their website since the release of their new album. I took an assignment from my module Introduction to Web Development as a chance to try and work out a better design for it. The website has four tabs. The Home page is supposed to introduce the topic of the website, and link to important external webpages like the social media accounts. The Tour page allows the viewer to get an overview of upcoming tour dates and the possibility of buying tickets from an external website. As 5SOS were not on tour when I was working on this assignment, I used old tour dates as an example. The Media page gives the viewer background information on the different 5SOS albums. Starting with the most recent one and ending with oldest. Next to every text is a picture carousel with five images. The About page gives the viewer a summary of who the band members are and what their role inside the band is. Flat Pack Lamp
This lamp was part of my final project in fist-year Design Studio. Only flat materials like paper and card were allowed to be used without any fixings. The shape of a jellyfish inspired the design of this lamp. Simple but graceful, the jellyfish form has a flowing design that conveys a feeling of elegance and fluidity. Curvilinear lines that flow naturally identify its shape. This project ran through five stages, inspiration, exploration, experimentation, concept development and the final submission. I hand-painted each lamp piece in aquamarine and silver to stimulate the colors of the sea, circling back to my original inspiration, the jellyfish.







Dragon Sculpture
The dragon sculpture was created by combining clay, paint, and 3D-printing. The sculpted dragon head is given life by its vivid green colors, and the 3D-printed horns' black paint with white sprinkles gives it an enchanting look. This sculpture is a fusion of traditional and contemporary art. I created this sculpture during my exchange year in the United States. As this was the second time I have worked with clay, I was and still am immensely proud of this sculpture.
